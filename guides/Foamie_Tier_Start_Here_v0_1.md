# Foamie Tier — Start Here

**v0.1 · 2026-07-08 · Slipstream open-source build series · Guide #0 (the beginner tier)**
**What this is:** the entry point to the Slipstream family — a foam-shell trailer you can build in ~50 hours with weekend tools for $1,100–$3,100 all-in. Slipstream doesn't reinvent the foamie: the tnttt.com community perfected it over 15+ years. This guide tells you exactly what to buy, which community plans to follow, and which parts NOT to build yourself. Numbers sourced in `docs/Slipstream_LowMid_Sourcing_v0_1.md`.

## The recipe in one paragraph

A Harbor Freight 4x8 folding trailer carries a shell built from 2" XPS foam board — no wood frame — glued with polyurethane glue, then skinned in **Poor Man's Fiberglass** (canvas bedded in Titebond II, sealed with exterior paint; the paint is structural, not cosmetic). Finished builds run 350–550 lb, tow behind literally any car, survive a decade outdoors, and dents heal with a heat gun. It is the cheapest real camping trailer that exists.

## What you buy vs what you build

**Buy these — they're where every first build fails:**

| Part | Why bought | Source | ~Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| Insulated door set (26x36 L+R) | Homemade doors warp and leak — #1 cited failure | Vintage Technologies (teardroptrailerparts.com) | $826 |
| RV windows (12x24 + 30x20) | Framing glass into curved foam walls | Vintage Tech / RecPro | ~$240 |
| Hurricane hinge, 62" | The galley-hatch hinge line is the classic leak point | RecPro | $52 |
| Vent fan | Sealed flange beats a hand-cut hole | MaxxFan Dome 12V | $85 |

**Build these — foam and glue is the whole point:** walls, roof (kerf-bent or laminated curves), galley, bed platform, interior.

## Shopping list (bare build ≈ $1,100)

- Harbor Freight 4x8 folding trailer #62666 (~$330–400; the 1,720 lb #58703 at $500 is its successor) + LED light kit $40 + safety chains $14
- 7× sheets 2" XPS (Foamular 250 class) ≈ $290
- PMF pack: ~13 yd 10 oz cotton duck ($120), 2 gal Titebond II ($50), Glidden Gripper ($24), exterior paint ($35)
- Adhesives: Gorilla Glue 36 oz ($28), Loctite PL300 foamboard tubes, foam-safe spray adhesive ≈ $60
- Manual roof vent ($46) if skipping the MaxxFan
- **Optional but recommended for highway use:** Dexter EZ-Lube 3,500 lb axle ($445) + Taskmaster radial tires ($205) — the stock HF bearings and bias tires are the known weak point

**Kit build (bought doors/windows/hinge/fan) ≈ $2,400–2,650. With axle upgrade ≈ $3,100.**

## Plans to follow (don't start from scratch)

1. **tnttt.com "Foamies" subforum** — the master thread ("foam builds") and the free **Widget PDF plans**: tnttt.com/threads/foamie-pdf-plans.1071612
2. **Instructables**: "Foamie Teardrop Camper" (13 steps) and "Mercury Foam Teardrop" (20 steps) — both free, both proven
3. **Overland Teardrop free 5x8 body plans**: overlandtrailer.com/learn/free-plans
4. **EzeInnovation CNC foam panel kit** if you want pre-cut panels (~$1–2K; doors/skin/glue not included — that's what the table above covers)

## Build-order crib sheet (details live in the plans)

Trailer prep (bearings, wiring, deck) → floor sandwich → walls up (PL300 + Gorilla, square-brace overnight) → roof (kerf-bend: cut half-depth kerfs, glue-fill, bend; or laminate 3× ¾" layers) → openings cut → **doors/windows/hinge installed per manufacturer instructions** → PMF skin (canvas into wet Titebond, squeegee, 2 coats, Gripper, 2+ paint coats) → galley + vent → interior.

**The three rules of foam:** (1) polyurethane glue for foam-to-foam, Titebond for canvas — never mix jobs; (2) weatherstrip adhesive won't stick over cured Gorilla Glue — mask door edges before gluing; (3) the paint layer is the waterproofing — recoat scuffs the same weekend you find them.

## What "Slipstream" adds at this tier

Honestly: curation, the failure-point parts list above, and an upgrade path. Your foamie's 12V basics (fridge, lights, MaxxFan, a folding solar panel) are the same parts the bigger tiers use, so nothing is wasted when you move up. Configure it in `configurator/Slipstream_Configurator.html` — pick **Build class: Foamie** and the BOM swaps to this tier's parts automatically.

## What this tier is not

No pop-up, no 48V spine, no composite shell, no propulsion — and no shame in that. A foamie that camps in August beats a flagship that ships in never.
